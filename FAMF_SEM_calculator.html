FAMF-SEM Calculator v2 — eigen signal · GCV · free φ

Computes the fixed method-loading pattern wi for a metadata-only Feature-Augmented Method Factor from item design features and the standardized residual correlation matrix of a baseline trait-only CFA. Signal = leading eigenvector of the residual matrix (signed); calibration = ridge regression with intercept, λ chosen by GCV; weights are scaled to Σwi2=k (convention) and fixed in the SEM while the method-factor variance φ is freely estimated. AMOS / LISREL / lavaan ready. Runs entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

1 Item metadata

One row per item, in the same order as your SEM. Reversed = 0/1, Polarity = +1 (positively keyed) / −1 (negatively keyed), Length = word count. Leave a feature blank/constant and it is ignored automatically.
Paste tab- or comma-separated rows: ID, Scale, Reversed, Page, Order, ScaleWidth, Polarity, Length (header row optional).

2 Residual correlation matrix

Paste the k×k standardized residual correlation matrix from your baseline trait-only CFA (same item order). Labels are ignored; the diagonal is not used.

3 Calibration

Under a single method factor the residual matrix is ≈ rank-one (wwT), so its leading eigenvector estimates w including signs — required for acquiescence-type effects on balanced scales.

    Method. Binary features (Reversed, Polarity) are effects-coded (centered); numeric features
    (Page, Order, ScaleWidth, Length) are standardized. Signal: m = v₁√max(ℓ₁,0) where (ℓ₁, v₁) is the
    leading eigenpair of the symmetrized residual correlation matrix (orientation: Σm ≥ 0); fallback:
    mi = mean |rresi·|. Ridge with intercept (unpenalized):
    γ̂ = (XTX + λD)−1XTm, X = [1 Z], D = diag(0,1,…,1).
    GCV(λ) = k⁻¹‖(I−H)m‖² / (1 − tr(H)/k)², H = X(XTX+λD)−1XT.
    Weights w = (γ₀+Zγ̂)·√(k/Σ(γ₀+Zγ̂)²) — scaled, not centered; if the prediction is
    degenerate the pattern collapses to equal weights (the equal-loading CLF). In the SEM the pattern
    is fixed and Var(M)=φ is estimated, so the data determine the method strength; φ̂≈0 indicates no
    detectable design-driven method variance. In LISREL all-Y models the latent covariance matrix is
    PS (Ψ), not PH (Φ).
